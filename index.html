Course overview

This course (and this accompanying website) are designed to provide you with a practical introduction to data management. It is a hands-on course in which you will get comfortable working with data, with a focus on data structures and database design. After completing this course, you hopefully will have a better understanding of how data infrastructures support the organizations, websites and applications that we use everyday, and feel confident in your ability to work with data and design effective databases that meet the needs of your users.

Schedule

The class time will be divided as follows:

  • 5:35 pm to 6:25 pm: lecture & Q&A in-person and on Teams (only on teams for January).

  • 6:35 am to 8:25 pm: Lab time with in-person and Teams support (only teams support for January)

In-person attendance will remain optional for the entire semester. When in-person teaching resumes, the lectures will be hybrid (in-person, with a recorded Teams broadcast). The labs will be in person, with virtual support available via Teams. Of course, I am always available for support outside of class hours and you can book a meeting with me here.
